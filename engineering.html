What I do

I'm a student of the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. In winter 2016, I'm (fingers crossed) graduating with a Bachelors of Engineering in Computer Science.

In 2016 I've been researching with the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA)1 in the Big Data for Environmental & Economic Policy group. We're looking into how feed forward neural nets and echo state networks can be used to classify and predict time series data using novel methods. I'm completing a senior thesis on this topic in the fall.

This summer, I'm working for Jump Trading as a development intern (think C++, memory allocators, and memoization).

Last summer I was an intern on the OS X Frameworks team, AppKit, with Apple in Cupertino, California. It was awesome, and I highly recommend the Apple intern experience.

I like to think that I have experience with most platforms, languages and domains; I've working on everything from graphics2 to interpreters3, VR4 to mobile5, data science to CS theory5. Learning is a favorite pastime of mine.
